# Dev Deepak — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, hiring managers, PMs, operators, and investors — people with high signal tolerance who visit once, scroll fast, and decide in seconds. They arrive having searched Dev's name or been sent the link. The job to be done: rapidly assess whether Dev is worth shortlisting or reaching out to. They should leave with one clear read: *this person is serious, technical, and unusually capable for their age.*

### Brand Personality
**Obsessive. Precise. Bold.**

Voice is confident without being brash. First-person, direct, no hedging. Specificity over hype — real numbers (35% attachment rate, 25% customer growth, £50M campaign) beat vague claims. The headline "Addicted to Hard Things" sets the register: competitive, self-aware, earned.

Emotional goal for visitors: arrive curious → scroll feeling the calibre shift → leave wanting to reach out or shortlist.

### Aesthetic Direction
Cinematic light. Premium agency energy. Apple.com meets Linear.app.

- Light only. Near-white base (#ffffff → #f5f5f7). No dark mode.
- Electric blue (#3563ff) as the primary accent — sharp, not washed out.
- Motion-heavy but purposeful: scroll-driven reveals, viewport triggers,
  spring physics. Framer Motion throughout.
- Typography: editorial and intentional. Avenir Next Condensed for display,
  tight tracking. Not templated.
- No generic AI portfolio aesthetics. No card soup. No floating orbs
  without purpose.
- Subtle depth through shadow and layering rather than glass panels —
  light surfaces, precise borders (border-black/8), elevation hierarchy.

**Company-flavoured sections** — each major work experience carries a subtle visual nod to that company's brand language. Transitions between sections should feel intentional — like changing channels. Each section is its own world; the overall site remains cohesive.

### Section Transition System
As the visitor scrolls through each company section, the visual language subtly shifts: accent colour, border character, spacing rhythm, and typographic weight all migrate together. The transition itself feels intentional — like changing channels. Implement via Framer Motion `useScroll` / `useTransform` or CSS custom property animation on section entry. Never abrupt, never invisible.

### Design Principles

1. **Specificity is the aesthetic.** Real numbers, real outcomes, real timelines. Never describe when you can quantify. "35% AppleCare attachment" > "top performer." Every claim earns its place.

2. **Company sections carry their own visual register.** Each employer section subtly shifts accent color, spacing rhythm, and typographic weight to echo that company's brand — not a copycat, a flavour. Visitors should *feel* the context change.

3. **Motion signals intent.** Animations are scroll-triggered and purposeful — they reveal hierarchy, not decoration. Heavy use of Framer Motion is expected, but every motion choice should feel earned and cinematic, never gratuitous.

4. **One section at a time, finished before the next.** Build section by section to completion. Never leave a section half-styled while moving on.

5. **Content lives in `lib/content.ts`.** All copy, stats, and data are centralised there. Components render; they don't hardcode content.
