# Dev Deepak — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
The audience is mixed: recruiters, hiring managers, founders, product leaders, and high-agency peers. Most visitors arrive from a name search, referral, or direct link and make a judgment quickly. The job to be done is to let them assess credibility, ambition, and range within one fast scan, then make it easy to remember Dev as someone worth shortlisting or reaching out to.

### Brand Personality
Ambitious. Capable. Driven.

The voice should feel direct, composed, and high-signal. Confident without sounding inflated. Specificity matters more than volume; evidence matters more than adjectives. The emotional goal is confidence: visitors should leave with the sense that this is someone serious, fast-moving, and able to operate above his tenure.

### Aesthetic Direction
Premium editorial product design. Apple.com meets Linear.app.

Borrow from Apple:
- restraint
- whitespace
- clarity of hierarchy
- polished surfaces
- careful motion

Borrow from Linear:
- precision
- rhythm
- product-grade typography
- dark-mode quality
- sharp but controlled interaction design

Avoid:
- corporate brand-site energy
- generic resume templates
- bloated card grids
- decorative effects without narrative purpose
- loud startup landing page cliches

The site should support both light and dark mode and follow the user's browser or system preference by default. The palette does not need to preserve any single existing accent color, but it should stay disciplined, high-contrast, and premium in both themes. Motion should remain purposeful and reduced-motion aware.

### Design Principles
1. Credibility before decoration. Every visual choice should strengthen trust, clarity, or memorability.
2. Scan fast, remember longer. The layout should communicate identity and trajectory within seconds, without feeling compressed.
3. Premium, not corporate. The interface should feel sharp and intentional, never like a finance dashboard or enterprise brochure.
4. Theme parity matters. Light and dark modes should both feel fully designed, not like one is a fallback.
5. Motion must earn its place. Use animation to reveal hierarchy, pacing, and polish, not to compete with the story.
